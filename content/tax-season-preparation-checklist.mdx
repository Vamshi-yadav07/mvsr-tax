## Get Organized Before Tax Season

Preparation is the key to a stress-free tax season. By gathering the right documents early and understanding what you need, you can save time, avoid errors, and potentially maximize your refund.

## Step 1: Gather Your Income Documents

- **W-2 forms** from all employers
- **1099-NEC / 1099-MISC** for freelance or contract work
- **1099-INT / 1099-DIV** for interest and dividends
- **1099-G** for unemployment compensation
- **1099-R** for retirement distributions
- **K-1 forms** for partnership or S-Corp income

## Step 2: Collect Deduction Documentation

- Mortgage interest statements (Form 1098)
- Property tax receipts
- Charitable donation receipts
- Medical expense records
- Student loan interest statements (Form 1098-E)
- Education expenses (Form 1098-T)

## Step 3: Organize Business Expenses (If Self-Employed)

- Income and expense ledger or bookkeeping records
- Business-related mileage log
- Home office measurements and expenses
- Equipment and supply receipts
- Health insurance premium statements

## Step 4: Review Life Changes

Major life events can significantly impact your taxes:

- **Marriage or divorce** — Filing status changes
- **New baby** — Additional dependent credits
- **Home purchase** — Mortgage interest and property tax deductions
- **Job change** — Multiple W-2s, moving expenses
- **Starting a business** — Business deductions and self-employment tax

## Step 5: Check Prior Year Returns

Review last year's return for:

- Carryforward items (capital losses, charitable contributions)
- State refund amounts reported as income
- Estimated tax payments made
- Depreciation schedules for business assets

## Step 6: Verify Personal Information

- Social Security numbers for all family members
- Bank account and routing numbers for direct deposit
- Prior year AGI (needed for e-filing)
- Identity Protection PIN (if issued by the IRS)
