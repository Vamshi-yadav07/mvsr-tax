## Avoid These Costly Mistakes

Filing your taxes correctly the first time saves you time, money, and stress. Here are the five most common mistakes we see at MVSR Tax — and how to avoid them.

## 1. Wrong Filing Status

Choosing the wrong filing status is one of the most frequent errors. Your filing status affects your tax rate, standard deduction, and eligibility for credits.

**Common mistake:** Filing as "Single" when you qualify for "Head of Household," which offers a larger standard deduction and lower tax rates.

**How to avoid it:** Review the IRS guidelines for each filing status or consult a tax professional.

## 2. Math Errors

Believe it or not, simple arithmetic mistakes are among the top reasons the IRS sends notices.

**How to avoid it:** Use tax software or a professional preparer. Double-check all calculations before submitting.

## 3. Missing Income

Forgetting to report income from a side job, freelance work, or investments can trigger an IRS notice.

**Common mistake:** Not reporting income from a 1099 because "it was just a small amount."

**How to avoid it:** The IRS receives copies of all your 1099s and W-2s. Report all income, no matter how small.

## 4. Not Claiming All Eligible Credits

Many taxpayers miss out on valuable credits like:

- Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)
- Child Tax Credit
- Education credits (American Opportunity, Lifetime Learning)
- Saver's Credit for retirement contributions

**How to avoid it:** Work with a tax professional who can identify all credits you're eligible for.

## 5. Filing Late Without an Extension

Missing the April 15 deadline without filing an extension results in a failure-to-file penalty of 5% per month on unpaid taxes.

**How to avoid it:** If you can't file on time, always file Form 4868 for an automatic six-month extension. Remember: an extension to file is NOT an extension to pay.
