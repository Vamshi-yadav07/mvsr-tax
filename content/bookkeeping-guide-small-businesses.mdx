## Why Bookkeeping Matters

Accurate bookkeeping isn't just about staying compliant with the IRS — it's a powerful tool for understanding your business's financial health, making informed decisions, and planning for growth.

## Getting Started: The Basics

### Choose Your Method

- **Cash basis:** Record transactions when money changes hands. Simpler, best for small businesses.
- **Accrual basis:** Record transactions when they're earned or incurred, regardless of payment. Required for businesses with over $25 million in revenue.

### Set Up Your Chart of Accounts

Organize your finances into categories:

- **Assets** — Bank accounts, equipment, inventory
- **Liabilities** — Loans, credit cards, accounts payable
- **Income** — Sales, service revenue, interest
- **Expenses** — Rent, utilities, supplies, payroll

## Essential Bookkeeping Tasks

### Daily

- Record all transactions
- Categorize expenses
- Save and file receipts

### Weekly

- Reconcile cash transactions
- Review accounts receivable
- Follow up on unpaid invoices

### Monthly

- Reconcile bank statements
- Review profit and loss statement
- Check accounts payable
- Review budget vs. actual spending

### Quarterly

- Prepare and pay estimated taxes
- Review financial statements
- Assess cash flow projections

### Annually

- Prepare year-end financial statements
- Organize documents for tax preparation
- Review and update your chart of accounts
- Plan for the upcoming year

## Best Practices

1. **Separate business and personal finances** — Open a dedicated business bank account and credit card
2. **Automate where possible** — Use accounting software like QuickBooks or Xero
3. **Keep all receipts** — Digital copies are acceptable; use apps to scan and store them
4. **Stay current** — Don't let transactions pile up; record them regularly
5. **Back up your data** — Use cloud-based solutions for automatic backups

## Common Bookkeeping Mistakes

- Mixing personal and business expenses
- Not tracking small cash transactions
- Failing to reconcile accounts monthly
- Ignoring accounts receivable
- Not keeping adequate documentation
