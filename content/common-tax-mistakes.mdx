## Why Mistakes Happen

Even careful filers can slip up when rules change or life gets busy. Here are five of the most common tax mistakes we see at MVSR Tax — and practical ways to avoid them.

## 1. Wrong or Outdated Filing Status

Your filing status affects brackets, credits, and the standard deduction. Picking the wrong one — or not updating it after a major life event — is a frequent source of errors.

**Tip:** Review IRS rules each year or work with a preparer who confirms your status before filing.

## 2. Math and Transcription Errors

Simple typos in income, withholding, or Social Security numbers can delay refunds or trigger notices.

**Tip:** Use software or e-file with validation, and reconcile totals against your W-2s and 1099s.

## 3. Missing Income

All taxable income must be reported, including side gigs, investment income, and platforms that issue 1099s.

**Tip:** Gather every tax document you receive; the IRS often has matching copies.

## 4. Overlooking Deductions and Credits

Many taxpayers miss education credits, retirement contributions, or business expenses they qualify for.

**Tip:** Keep receipts and ask a professional whether you are leaving money on the table.

## 5. Missing Deadlines or Extension Rules

Late filing without an extension, or misunderstanding extension rules (an extension to file is not an extension to pay), can mean penalties and interest.

**Tip:** Mark key dates, file for an extension if needed, and estimate taxes owed when you extend.
